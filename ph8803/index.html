Physics 8803 -- Cosmology

This course is an introduction to cosmology. This is aimed mainly at 1st and 2nd year graduate students interested in CCAPP, but students from all research specialties are welcome.

I will assume as background the standard advanced undergraduate courses (E&M, quantum, statistical mechanics). I will not assume prior knowledge of general relativity or observational astronomy (I will introduce some concepts from these areas as needed).

The course will meet Wednesdays and Fridays, 9:35-10:55 AM, in Caldwell 119. The first class will be held on Wednesday, January 9.

Topics covered will include the following. We may explore some topics in more or less depth depending on student interest.

  • The expanding Universe; redshifts, distance measurements, cosmic acceleration, etc.
  • Thermal history of the Universe: Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, recombination, the cosmic microwave background.
  • Linear perturbations in cosmology: inflationary models of the origin of perturbations, CMB anisotropies, large scale distribution of matter
  • Non-linear structure: formation and history of galaxies, galaxy clusters, and the intergalactic medium
  • Observational aspects of cosmology surveys
